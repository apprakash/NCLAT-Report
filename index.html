NCLAT Data Analysis Report

Executive Summary

This comprehensive report analyzes case listings and disposals over a 5-year period, with a focus on Insolvency, Competition law, and Company matters. The analysis addresses key questions regarding daily new listings, disposal rates, and time to disposal for different case types.

Key Findings

  • A total of 11,473 cases were listed over the 5-year period.
  • The daily average of new cases listed is 7.51 cases.
  • Of the total cases, 8,054 cases (70.2%) have been disposed of.
  • Insolvency matters represent 84.36% of all cases, making them the most frequently heard type.
  • The average time to dispose of cases varies significantly by case type:
    • Insolvency matters: 321 days
    • Competition law: 427 days
    • Company matters: 358 days

Conclusion

This comprehensive analysis of case filings and disposals over a 5-year period reveals several important patterns:

  • The tribunal handles an average of 7.51 new cases daily, with a total of 11,473 cases over the 5-year period.
  • Insolvency matters dominate the caseload, accounting for 84.36% of all cases, followed by Company matters (14.53%) and Competition law cases (1.11%).
  • The overall disposal rate is 70.2%, with 8,054 cases disposed of out of the total 11,473 cases.
  • The average time to disposal varies by case type, with Competition law cases taking the longest (427 days) and Insolvency matters being resolved most quickly (321 days).

These findings provide valuable insights for resource allocation, case management strategies, and policy decisions aimed at improving the efficiency and effectiveness of the tribunal's operations.
